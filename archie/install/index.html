The Desktop App

Install Archie

Archie runs on your own computer — it isn’t a website you log into. Download it, open it, and your agent lives on your machine. Archie hasn’t shipped yet (here’s where it stands), so read this page as a preview: join the waitlist and this is where we’ll send you on release day. Below is where each platform’s download will live, and an honest account of what you’ll see the first time you open it.

Mac

macOS 12 Monterey or later. One download works on both Apple silicon and Intel Macs.

Download for Mac

Signed and notarized with Apple. It opens the way any other app does — no warnings, nothing to click past.

Windows

Windows 10 or 11, 64-bit.

Download for Windows

Windows will try to stop you the first time. That’s expected with our first releases, and the next section explains exactly why — please read it before you install.

Windows

“Windows protected your PC”

When you open the installer, Windows will show a blue box with that heading, and the only obvious button will say Don’t run. Nothing is wrong. Here is how to get past it, and then why it happens at all — because you shouldn’t click through a security warning just because someone told you to.

1

Click “More info”

It’s the small link in the blue box, above the buttons. It’s easy to miss — it doesn’t look like a link.

2

Check the publisher

Two lines appear. The app name should read Archie. If it says anything else, stop — you don’t have our installer.

3

Click “Run anyway”

A new button appears once you’ve expanded More info. Archie installs normally from there, and you won’t see this screen again on this machine.

Why Windows says that

Windows trusts an app when its maker has bought a code-signing certificate — and it charges for that trust. The warning isn’t Windows detecting anything in Archie. It’s Windows telling you, accurately, that it doesn’t yet recognize who made it.

We’re a small team, and that certificate is on the list for when we’re further along. Until we buy it, every Windows build we ship shows this screen. We’d rather tell you that plainly than dress it up.

How to check us, not trust us

You don’t have to take our word for it. The download link on this page points at our public releases page on GitHub, where you can see every build we’ve ever published, when it went out, and how many people have downloaded it.

On the Mac we do hold Apple’s developer certificate, and that build is signed and notarized — the same team, the same code, checked by Apple. If you have both machines, install the Mac one first and see for yourself.

Yours Alone

Your Keys Never Reach Us

Archie asks for an AI key so your agent can think, and that key belongs to you, on an account you own and pay. It is stored in your operating system’s own vault — your Mac’s Keychain, or Windows Credential Manager — the same place your computer keeps its passwords. We have no way to read it. Your agent talks to the AI directly from your machine, so your conversations never pass through us and we hold no copy of them.

What stays on your computer

  • Your AI keys and connected accounts
  • Your agent, its instructions, and its memory
  • Your files, notes, and knowledge base
  • Every message you and your agent exchange

What Otian’s servers hold

  • Your email address, so you can sign in
  • Whether your subscription is active
  • Which add-ons you’ve bought
  • Nothing else — no chats, no files, no keys

There is no analytics or tracking in Archie. If it ever crashes it writes a log, and that log stays on your machine unless you choose to send it to us.
