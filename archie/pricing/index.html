Pricing

What Archie Costs

Nothing here is for sale yet — these are the prices Archie will launch with, told to you now so there are no surprises later. Here’s where it stands.

Running It Yourself

Three Costs. That’s the Whole List.

One of them is ours. The other two are yours, paid straight to the people providing them — and we never take a cut of either.

The App
$149one-time

Buy Archie once and own it — no subscription. The version you buy is yours to keep.

Add-ons
Freeto start

Every kind — Skills, Workers, Routines, Personalities — ships with free built-ins. Premium ones are a one-time purchase, never a second subscription.

The AI Itself
Pay as you go

Your own account with Anthropic or OpenAI. It bills you directly, and the money never passes through us.

What the AI costs is genuinely up to you, because it depends on how hard you work your agent. Ask it a handful of things a day and it’s a few dollars a month. Lean on it constantly — long research, big documents, all-day back-and-forth — and it climbs into the tens of dollars. You can watch the meter in real time on the provider’s own dashboard, because the account is yours, not ours.

A few add-ons reach out to services that charge their own fees. When one does, it tells you before you install it — so the number you expect is the number you pay.

And if Archie can’t do something you need, we’ll build the add-on. That’s quoted per job, scoped and priced before any work starts — and you earn 25% of the proceeds from every future sale of it.

What It’s Worth

Put It Next to the Alternative

The job you’re handing your agent — the inbox, the calendar, the follow-ups, the research, the small things that eat a week — is the job you’d otherwise hand a person.

A part-time personal assistant can run thousands of dollars a year. They also go home at five, take holidays, and have to be told the same thing twice.

Your agent doesn’t. It’s there at six in the morning when you want the day laid out, and at eleven at night when you remember the thing you forgot — for as long as your computer is on and Archie is running.

That’s the trade: tens of dollars a month, for help that never clocks out.
