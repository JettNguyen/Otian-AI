How It Works

One Agent Per Teammate, on Their Own Machine

Archie is our desktop app for building personal AI agents. In a business, that means each person gets an agent shaped to their role — not one shared bot in somebody else’s cloud.

Shaped to Each Role

The person on support gets an agent that triages the inbox and drafts replies. The owner gets one that chases follow-ups and preps the weekly numbers. Same app, different agents — each running on its owner’s computer.

We Never Hold Your Data

Customer lists, invoices, inboxes — your business data stays on your machines, in your custody. Your agents talk to the AI company directly on your own accounts, so nothing your team works on passes through us. Our servers hold three things: each teammate’s email, whether they own a license, and which paid add-ons were bought. No customer data, no documents, no conversations.

It Can’t Email Your Customers or Spend Your Money

Archie can draft and send email and change a connected calendar — but only after you approve that exact action: it proposes, you approve, then it acts. It has no ability to spend money or make a purchase — that doesn’t exist in the app. And an agent speaks only in the chats you connect it to, to teammates you’ve approved. We keep the honest, current list on our Trust page.

Guided Setup

Rather Have Us Walk Your Team Through It?

You can set all of this up yourself — Archie is built for that. But plenty of owners would rather have someone alongside them the first time. That’s our guided setup: we get on a call with you and your team, build the agents together on your machines, connect accounts with your approval, test everything, and fine-tune after launch. The full process is laid out on How It Works.

No two businesses need the same thing. One owner wants a single agent for replies and scheduling; another has five teammates, a dozen tools, and ten years of process. A flat rate would leave one overpaying and the other underserved — so we price by the session instead. A session is $100 and runs an hour. The rate never goes up because a setup is complicated; a bigger setup simply takes more sessions, and we tell you how many on the free discovery call.

Archie is a one-time $149 license per person when it ships — a separate cost from guided setup, not a subscription. The AI thinking itself runs on each person’s own pay-as-you-go account with Anthropic or OpenAI — typically a few dollars a month per person, billed directly by the provider — and we’ll help each teammate get set up.
