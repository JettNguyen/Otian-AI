The Basics

Think of it as a personal helper that works for you around the clock. You message it like you'd text a friend, and it handles the task: checking your email, setting reminders, looking things up, and more. It's not a chatbot with canned responses. It actually does things.
None, and coding is entirely optional. We teach and guide you through setup in plain language, building on your own computer together — you can stay hands-off, hands-on, or somewhere in between. If you ever want to learn how it works under the hood, we’ll go as deep as you like at your own pace. If you’d rather not, that’s completely fine too.
This is something we lead with, not something that happens by accident. Setup isn't just us installing software and handing you a finished product - it's a real back-and-forth where we walk you through what the technology can and can't do, and why we're making each choice. You don't need to become technical, but you should walk away understanding your own assistant, not just using it. The best results come when a client understands the tool well enough to keep imagining new things for it to do - so before we build anything, we sit down together and shape a vision for what your assistant should become. The scope of work comes out of that conversation, not the other way around.

Cost & Billing

$100 per guided session. Your first one is a free 30-minute consultation - we'll talk through your goals, what your assistant should look like, and a rough timeline. No cost, no obligation. If you decide to move forward, sessions from there are $100 each.
Yes, and it’s small and separate from our service fee — though how it works depends on which path you’re on. If we’re setting up your assistant together through our guided service, you’ll want a standard AI subscription (like Claude Pro or ChatGPT Plus, usually around $20 per month) to work with during the build. If you’re using the Archie app, it connects to the AI through your own API key instead — a pay-as-you-go account where you only pay for what your assistant actually does. Each message costs anywhere from a fraction of a cent to a few cents, so a typical personal assistant runs just a few dollars a month. You add a little credit up front (say $5–$20), it draws down slowly, and you can set a hard spending cap on the provider’s website. Inside Archie you can also dial each assistant between Economy, Balanced, and Best models, so you decide the balance of cost and quality.
They’re two different products from the same company. A Claude or ChatGPT subscription only works on that company’s own website and app — it can’t be used by other programs. Your Archie assistant runs on your own computer, so it connects to the AI through a separate doorway called an API, which bills on its own based on actual use. The upside: for most people the API costs less than a monthly subscription, because you only pay for the messages your assistant actually sends — often just a few dollars a month — and you stay in full control of the account and the spending. Your existing Claude Pro or ChatGPT Plus plan keeps working as normal; the API is simply what lets a program you own talk to the AI on your behalf.
An API key is like a password that lets your assistant use the AI on your behalf. Because your assistant lives on your computer — not on our servers — it talks to the AI company directly, with your key. That means you always own the account, you see exactly what’s spent, and your conversations never pass through us. Getting a key takes about two minutes, and the app walks you through every step in plain language.

Safety & Privacy

Only the ones you approve. We go through everything with you before connecting anything, and you can revoke access at any time. Nothing gets linked without your explicit sign-off.
By default, your assistant never takes an irreversible action (sending a message, making a purchase) without your approval first. You control how much independence it has, and we can adjust that together later if you decide to trust it with more.
Nothing is permanent - if something goes wrong, we fix or undo it, then fine-tune based on your feedback. Early mistakes are a normal, expected part of the process; that's what the tuning period is for.
No. Archie runs entirely on your own computer, and your assistant’s instructions, uploaded files, chat history, and API key all stay on your Mac — the key is kept in the Mac’s built-in Keychain and never leaves your device. Everything lives in a private folder just for Archie; if you remove the app, that data goes with it. Otian AI does not store, read, or have any access to it. The only thing our servers know is whether your subscription is active (your email and payment status) so we can let you into the app. Nothing else.

Timeline & What to Expect

A first working version can often be live the same day, and most clients have one to three useful workflows running within the first week of completing the intake form — you don’t need to wait for a perfect final version to start benefiting. From there, “setup” isn’t really a finish line. Most people keep building for weeks and months, adding accounts, tasks, and new ways of using it as they go.
Yes. This is the recommended path. Start with one or two high-value workflows, then add more once those are stable and saving you time.
There’s no real ceiling — if it can be done on a computer or through an app, your assistant can probably learn to do it. Inbox and calendar, sure, but also research, bookkeeping, travel, scheduling, smart-home control, health tracking, customer follow-ups, even building you a small tool or website. Day to day it looks different for everyone: for some it’s a morning briefing and an inbox that stays clean; for others it’s quietly running parts of a business — fielding customer messages, managing schedules, keeping projects moving. Some people talk to theirs constantly; others let it work in the background and check in occasionally. The real question isn’t “can it do X,” it’s “what would make your life or business easier” — and from there, almost anything is on the table.
We handle the technical side completely, and once it’s running, it mostly takes care of itself. The more interesting part isn’t technical — it’s a conversation about what you want your assistant trusted with, and that goes as far as you want: some clients keep it to one or two things done well, others keep expanding its responsibilities month after month. Either way, we stay available with ongoing guidance — from quick check-ins to deeper working sessions — whenever you want to adjust something or add the next capability.

Practical Details

Your assistant runs on a small dedicated computer — a Mac Mini — that sits in your home or office and stays on around the clock. We help you source one if you don't have it yet, walk you through the setup over Zoom, and handle everything from there.
Everything you built stays on your computer, but the app locks until you subscribe again. Any skills you purchased are yours to keep. Your AI provider account and its key are completely separate and unaffected — you manage those directly with Anthropic or OpenAI.
